Borscht

back to recipe list

Description

A hearty beet soup characterized by it's deep red colour and slightly sweet taste. Typically made with a bouillon base and a multitude of vegetables. Sometimes served with uszka: small, ear-shaped Polish dumplings filled with minced meat or wild mushrooms.

The following recipe is in traditional Ukrainian style, without meat and using chicken broth. Add a combination of chopped and shredded vegetables to create variety.

Ingredients

  • 3 medium beets, peeled, chopped or shredded
  • 3 carrots, peeled, chopped or shredded
  • 3 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 medium head of cabbage, cored and shredded
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes, drained
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1/2 (6 ounce) can tomato paste
  • 1 cube chicken bouillon
  • 1 can chicken stock
  • 8 cups water, or as needed
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • vinegar to taste
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup sour cream, for topping
  • fresh dill, for topping

Steps

  1. Fill a large pot halfway with water (about 8 cups). Add chicken bouillon and chicken stock. Bring to a boil.
  2. Add beets and cook until they have lost their color. Add carrots and potatoes and simmer until tender, about 15 minutes.
  3. Add cabbage and diced tomatoes to pot.
  4. Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add onion and cook until tender. Stir in tomato paste and 3/4 cup water until well blended. Transfer to the pot.
  5. Add garlic to the soup, cover, and turn off the heat. Let stand 5 minutes. Stir in vinegar and season with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve with sour cream and dill.
